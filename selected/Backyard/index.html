Welcome to our humble abode. A half acre yard filled with trees, creatures, and peaceful times.

I hope you enjoy this little gallery compiled of pictures taken from the deck.

Name: Spaz; this Eastern Grey Squirrel is one of the more hyperactive visitors to our deck. A bit jumpy, but such a cutie.
Name: Baldy; A beautiful bald eagle soars over the yard. In my haste to capture the photo, none of the settings were ready and this blurred image was the best I could grab before it sailed away.
After having the bright idea of hunching under an awning during a thunderstorm, this cool shot wasn't even realized until my fiancee and I went back into the house and started going through our shots.
The moon in full glory hovering just a few degrees above the treeline in the backyard.
A random Common Grackle feeds at the base of one of several feeders dotted around the yard.
Name: Bunbun; one of the cutest little bunnies that feed off of our poorly kept backyard. The patches of clovers all over are like a buffet to these chubsters.
Name: Alan; A running joke between my fiance and myself is that this Fox Squirrel isn't begging for peanuts, but calling out a friend's name that he can't quite remember.
Name: LL Blue Jay; This lovely little blue jay comes along every morning and chills out by a near by tree and waits for me to toss out some peanuts before snatching a couple and flying away in a swift, lovely blue blur.
It generally seems that we're more likely to find several different types of wasps and hornets hanging out by the hummingbird feeders than seeing the darty little Ruby-throats.
Name: Chippy Chip; one of the first chipmunks that we were able to hand feed, Chippy is quite the adorable little fellow. Until Chippy accidentally bites your finger thinking it's a peanut. It's just a little nibble, though.
These colorful little finches not only bring a pretty dash of color to the yard, their chirps are often a pleasant start to the morning.
Taken after only a few seconds of exposure and not properly processed, it's still pretty fascinating that you can spot galaxies and nebula from the yard, despite being near so many large cities.
An adorable little bumble bee bounces around the yard, pulling pollen from a weedy ground-cover plant.
Name: Big Chunkers; This fellow is the biggest of the Fox Squirrels and yet, the most docile. Often bullied by the smaller grey squirrels, Chunkers prefers to grab one peanut at a time, crack it open, and ponder life's greatest mysteries from the deck railings.
Name: Roger; Roger rabbit is a kind and funny little guy. Running just far enough away from me to be out of reach, Roger will sometimes trot around behind me as I pick up after the yard.
These black capped chickadees are often found swarming the bird feeders in a cacophony of chirps and tweets. They get a long with their fellow tree sparrows, but usually them immediately darting away is the sign of a red-tailed hawk overhead...or me being too close.
That smudge highlighted in the picture is a raw shot of M31 - The Andromeda Galaxy. Barely visible with the naked eye, it's only on clear dark nights that we can see it's hazy appearance, either through binoculars or one of our small telescopes.
Name: Screech; This feisty little bluejay is notorious for constantly screeching the entire time its on the deck. Even after stuffing its mouth, Screech soars away with a now hilariously muffled call.
Name: Flower; A quite goofy little chipmunk. When I'm hand-feeding Flower, all is well. The moment I leave out a bowl of seeds or a dish with water, I can usually guess what culprit has flipped the object.
Name: Steve; Intense stare-downs aside, Steve is pretty sweet. The only other Fox Squirrel besides Chunkers that'll take food from my hand, Steve prefers to chill at the end of the deck, depositing his peanut shells in a pile at the base of the deck, much to the chagrin of me having to clean them up.
Name: Van-Gogh; Named after noticing the chip in his ear, Van-Gogh is violent. Often thrashing and chasing each and every other squirrel that comes around, his penchant for chaos is only satisfied after realizing he can't carry more than two peanuts at once.
A quirky little hummingbird sips nectar from one of the generic feeders dotted around the yard. The dollar store version attracts the most activity.
Name: Neowise; An interstellar visitor that caught the eyes of astronomers and stargazers alike, we were lucky to be able to capture this faint object hovering low in the sky in the month of August 2020.
Name: Bart; this Eastern Grey Squirrel stole our hearts. One of the first squirrels to make a habit of greeting us every morning in exchange for snacks, she'll often times snack away at this little squirrel made dining table whilst we stare at our laptops enjoying the sun and sounds.
